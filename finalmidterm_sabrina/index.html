Greetings!
Who does not like food?

When it comes to food, Bangladeshi food is one of the most popular foods in asian continent. It is made up of a diverse range of delicious spices, herbs, rice, fish meats and breads. Consisting of rich, creamy, spicy, flavorsome curries and daal, the food in Bangladesh is comparable to Indian food due to the fact that they share a border, a history of British rule, and cultural similarities including a love of spicy food. Bangladesh is a majority Muslim country which means the Bangladeshi diet is strictly halal and meat is more common. Some of the famous Bangladeshi dishes are given in here, not just delicacies in Bangladeshi people, but often a big hit in Western countries too.

Fish Curry

Bangladesh is a land of fish and rice. This fish curry is immensely popular and traditionally cooked with a freshwater fish called Rohui. It’s available frozen in Bangladeshi grocery shops. Ruhi fish curry is really easy to make and healthier, packed with flavor & spice, and ready in less than 30 minutes. It is usually served with boiled white rice. Beef Curry

In Bangladesh meat is a staple with Beef being the most popular meat followed by Chicken. This Beef curry dish takes a bit of preparation but the end result is super delicious! The flavor of Bangladeshi Beef curry is quite different from Indian or Thai curry. Bangladeshi Beef curry is thicker,hot and spicy and always served with rice or roti. It can be made on the stovetop, in a slow cooker, or in a pressure cooker. Kids be warned: the sauce for this beef curry is a little bit spicy.More

Desserts

When it comes to Bengali sweets, whether you have a sweet tooth or not hardly matters. One look at these enticing babies will melt your heart and your taste buds! Bangladeshi desserts owe their origins to Western Bengali influences – and the sweets and confectionery of the eastern region of Bengali are among the most delicious across all of Asia. The popularity of such desserts and sweet snacks (mishti) is widespread, and includes recipes such as faluda (fruit custard), Tiler sandesh, patishapta pitha and gulab jamun mishti, rasmalai, payesh, doi etc..
